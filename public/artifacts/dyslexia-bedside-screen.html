Outpatient Pediatrics · Clinical Screen

Bedside Dyslexia
Screening Tool

5–6 yrs Kindergarten Phoneme awareness · Letter sounds ▼
Task 1
Letter Sound Knowledge
Say to child"I'm going to show you some letters. Tell me what sound each one makes — not the name, the sound. Ready?"

Point to each letter one at a time: S · M · T · F · B
S M T F B

By end of kindergarten: 4–5/5 correct

Concern: ≤2/5, or gives letter names instead of sounds consistently

Task 2
Phoneme Segmentation (Spoken Words)
Say to child"I'm going to say a word, and I want you to tap once for every sound you hear. Like this — 'sun' has three sounds: /s/ /u/ /n/. Now you try."

Words to use: cat · up · fish · hop · bell

Expected: 3–4 taps correct on 3+ words

Concern: Taps syllables instead of phonemes, or can only do 1-sound words

This age: segmentation of 2–3 phoneme words is the benchmark. Don't use blends (st-, bl-) at this age.

Task 3
Rhyme Recognition
Say to child"Do these words rhyme? Cat — bat." (pause) "Dog — log." (pause) "Sun — cup."

Expected: Correctly identifies first two as rhymes, third as non-rhyme.

Expected: 2/3 correct minimum

Concern: Cannot identify any rhyme pairs; confuses rhyme with same first sound

6–8 yrs Early Elementary Decoding · Nonsense words · RAN ▼
Task 1 · Core Test
Nonsense Word Reading
Say to child"These are made-up words — they're not real, so you've never seen them before. Sound them out the best you can."
bim frop stavel nust cheld widge

Expected (1st–2nd grade): 4–5/6 decoded correctly or with close approximation

Concern: Guesses real words ("frog" for "frop"), refuses, or produces phonologically distant responses

This is your highest-yield single item. No whole-word memory to fall back on — pure phonological decoding.

Task 2
Phoneme Segmentation — Harder
Say to child"Tell me every sound you hear in each word. Go slowly."

Words: ship · flat · blast · chin

ship → /sh/ /i/ /p/  |  flat → /f/ /l/ /a/ /t/  |  blast → /b/ /l/ /a/ /s/ /t/

Concern: Merges blends (says "fl" as one sound), misses final consonants, collapses clusters

Task 3
Rapid Automatized Naming (Informal)
Say to child"Name these as fast as you can without stopping." Point across the row quickly.
2 7 4 9 1 5 3 8

Expected: Smooth, fast, minimal hesitation

Concern: Slow, hesitant, skips, self-corrects frequently — especially combined with poor nonsense word reading (double-deficit pattern)

8–11 yrs Late Elementary Irregular words · Spoonerisms · Fluency ▼
Task 1
Irregular Word Reading (Surface Dyslexia Probe)
Say to child"Read each word aloud. Take your time."
yacht sword colonel island receipt

Expected: Recognizes whole-word forms, doesn't over-regularize

Surface pattern: "yatched," "col-o-nel," "re-CEIPT" — phonics applied where it fails

Phonological dyslexics often do better here (they've memorized common words). Surface dyslexics fail these specifically.

Task 2 · Highest Demand
Spoonerisms
Say to child"This is a word game. I'll say two words and you swap the first sounds. Like: 'top hat' becomes 'hop tat.' Ready?"

Pairs: cat / dog  →  dat/cog
big / sun  →  sig/bun
fish / map  →  mish/fap

Expected (age 8+): 2–3/3 correct with some processing time

Concern: Cannot do any correctly; substitutes semantically related words; freezes

This is the most cognitively demanding phonological manipulation task. Strong ceiling effect — kids who succeed here almost certainly don't have phonological dyslexia.

Task 3
Advanced Nonsense Words
Say to child"Made-up words again — sound them out."
phlomit scrandel quivest throstip

Expected: Phonologically close decoding, handles consonant clusters and digraphs

Concern: Still guessing real words, omits syllables, cannot handle multi-syllable nonsense words

Clinical Decision Guide

Refer for Full Eval Fails nonsense words + phoneme segmentation at age level. Any double-deficit pattern (poor RAN + poor decoding). Parent/teacher concern + 1 failed task.
Monitor Closely Borderline performance on 1 task. Strong family history. Reading avoidance despite adequate instruction. Oral language clearly outpaces written.
Reassure + Recheck Age-appropriate on all tasks. Concern appears to be pacing or attention rather than phonological deficit. Recheck in 6 months if worry persists.

This is a clinical screen, not a diagnostic instrument. Formal diagnosis requires psychoeducational evaluation (CTOPP-2, TOWRE-2, WRMT-III). Refer to neuropsychology or developmental pediatrics for confirmed concerns.
